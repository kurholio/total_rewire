Knob-and-Tube Rewiring for Safer, Modern Living

The first standardized electrical system in the U.S. — served faithfully from the 1880s through the 1940s. Today, it’s obsolete and no longer able to support modern electrical loads. Current electrical codes prohibit tapping into existing K&T systems, and most insurance companies raise red flags at the sight of exposed wiring in garages or attics. Rewiring often requires opening walls, which we carefully patch and paint with respect for those living in the home. It’s also a great time to add recessed lighting in your kitchen or living room, and install additional outlets where needed.

  • Breathe New Life into Your Home’s Electrical
  • Modern Wiring for Modern Living

Code Compliance Without the Overhaul

We help building owners meet today’s electrical codes and insurance requirements — which in some cases, don’t require a full service upgrade. Our team ensures that all upgrades, big or small, are done cleanly, safely, and with minimal disruption to your tenants or operations.
